# Source Notes For Straddie Maker-Space Lab

Generated for the public prototype site. This note keeps the source trail readable without exposing local Windows paths.

## Main Public Framing

The site keeps the public story centred on a practical, public access light-industrial maker-space in Dunwich / Goompi:

- make useful things
- fix and maintain everyday objects
- recycle and upcycle materials
- share tools carefully through visible stewardship and check-out rules
- learn safe tool use
- test small local material ideas
- document work through hyperlocal media and noticeboards
- connect to Ready S.E.T. Co-op workforce pathways
- hold Sandworm / subterranean systems as future-horizon research, not a starter promise

## Main Ideas Carried Into The Site

- The maker-space should be public-facing and useful before it becomes impressive.
- The Try Everything Once workforce theory works best as small, safe, supervised rotations through real tasks.
- Ready S.E.T. Co-op can be framed as a people, training and contribution pathway.
- Hyperlocal media and digital noticeboards can turn practical work into public knowledge.
- Disaster kiosks can make the same public information layer useful during ordinary days and pressure events.
- Shared Table / modern Dig for Australia links the maker-space to gardens, pantries, preserving, food rescue and trusted local sharing.
- Grants Lab can turn small practical experiments into clearer funding evidence.
- Tool sharing needs a public-safe record pattern: steward, availability, induction, care rules, consumables, safety and pause conditions.
- Markdown form builders borrow from the Straddie Content Assets Kit / film-club shared-assets pattern, then adapt it for what people want to learn, build, repair, experiment with, share or offer as materials before those notes become public.
- A Tip Loop sister-site could explore lawful pre-tip diversion, not unauthorised dump salvage.
- Redland City Council lists the North Stradbroke Island Recycling and Waste Centre at 100 East Coast Road, and its RecycleWorld page gives a mainland reuse precedent at Redland Bay.
- Recent councillor posts reportedly mention possible additional recycling facilities for Straddie; this is treated as a lead to verify until a public post or Council record is linked.
- A concrete batch-plant sister-site could map concrete, recycled inputs, non-structural tests, safe tool sharing and low-waste material literacy if owners, operators and qualified reviewers wanted to explore it.
- Local sand and mineral-sand ideas are rich, but public wording needs strong boundaries around law, culture, Country, environment and safety.
- Subterranean / Sandworm systems belong on the future map as accountable questions, not near-term claims.
- The compact tool-shed playlist supports the idea that a small room can hold serious capability when storage, labelling and workflow are designed well.
